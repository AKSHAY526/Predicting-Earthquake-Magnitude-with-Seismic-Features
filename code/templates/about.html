Introduction

Earthquakes are natural phenomena caused by the sudden release of energy in the Earth's crust, resulting in seismic waves. They can vary in magnitude and can have significant impacts on human lives and infrastructure.

Earthquake Related Images

The location below the earth's surface where the earthquake starts is called the hypocenter, and the location directly above it on the surface of the earth is called the epicenter.



P waves, or Primary waves, are the first waves to arrive at a seismograph. P waves are the fastest seismic waves and can move through solid, liquid, or gas. They leave behind a trail of compressions and rarefactions on the medium they move through



S waves, also known as secondary waves, shear waves, or shaking waves, are a type of elastic wave that move through the body of an object. They are one of the two main types of elastic body waves

Dataset Information

The dataset contains information about earthquakes, including various attributes such as time, latitude, longitude, depth, magnitude, and more.

Dataset Columns

Column Description
time Timestamp of the earthquake
latitude Latitude of the earthquake's epicenter
longitude Longitude of the earthquake's epicenter
depth Depth of the earthquake in kilometers
mag Magnitude of the earthquake
nst Number of seismic stations used to determine earthquake location
gap Horizontal distance between epicenter and nearest seismic station
dmin Horizontal distance to nearest seismic station
rms Root mean square value of amplitude measurements
net Network that recorded the earthquake
id Unique identifier for the earthquake event
updated Timestamp when the earthquake information was last updated
place Location description of the earthquake
type Type of earthquake event
horizontalError Horizontal error of the earthquake location
depthError Depth error of the earthquake location
magError Magnitude error of the earthquake
magNst Number of seismic stations used to determine magnitude
status Status of the earthquake event
locationSource Source of the earthquake location information
magSource Source of the earthquake magnitude information

Independent Variables and Ranges

  • Latitude and Longitude:
    • Latitude: -90 to 90 degrees (covering the entire range of latitudes).
    • Longitude: -180 to 180 degrees (covering the entire range of longitudes).
  • Depth:
    • 0 to 700 kilometers.
  • nst (Number of reporting stations):
    • range of 0 to 100.
  • gap (Azimuthal gap):
    • 0 to 360 degrees
  • dmin (Minimum distance to earthquake):
    • 0 to 100 km
  • rms (Root Mean Square):
    • 0 to 2
  • horizontalError, depthError, magError, magNst:
      • horizontalError: 0 to 100 km.
      • depthError: 0 to 100 km.
      • magError: 0 to 1.
      • magNst: 0 to 100.

Dependent Variable

Magnitude (mag): The magnitude of the earthquake, typically ranging from 0 to 10
